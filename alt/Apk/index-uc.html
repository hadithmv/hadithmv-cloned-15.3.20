Translators Preface


. . . .

- In the Name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful -

All praise (and thanks) is for Allah, the Rabb (ie: Lord) of the Aalameen (ie: creation). And Peace and Blessings be upon the Allahs Messenger, and upon all of his family and companions. O Allah, there is no ease except for what You make easy, and You make the difficult, if you Will, easy. Exalted are you (from having any imperfections), we have no knowledge except what you have taught us, indeed you are the Knowing, the Wise.

Imam Nawawi was a great scholar of the Shafii Mazhab and a famous Imam. His name is Yahya bin Sharaf bin Murri bin Hasan bin Hussain bin Muhammad bin Jumuah bin Hizam. His Kunya is Abu Zakariyya. He was born on Muharram 631 H. (October 1233 AD.) in the city of Nawa, a place near Damascus, Syria. His title an-Nawawi refers to his hometown.

Nawawi was blessed with sharp intelligence and the love of learning. Even at the age of 10, Nawawi was not interested in playing like the rest of the children. They would try to force him to play, and he would cry and flee from them, devoting his time to reciting the Quran. Nawawi memorized the Quran before reaching puberty. When he was 18, his father took him to Damascus to study there, which was the center of scholars and learning at that time. He would stay in a very small room filled with many books. He spent all his time seeking knowledge such that he would not even sleep except when it overtook him. He would fall asleep on his books, then awaken and continue studying. He never married. He was so busy attaining knowledge that that he would attend 12 lectures a day with his teachers on various Islamic sciences, not to mention his reviewing and memorizing of what he learned. Nawawi lived a simple and austere life. Eating only what he knew to be Halal and avoiding everything doubtful. He would even teach without accepting a salary. He was given the title Muhyiddeen (ie: reviver of the religion), although he hated being called as such out of modesty.

During his 45 years of life, he authored many books covering various Islamic sciences, such as: 40 Hadith, Riyad as Saaliheen, al-Minhaj (Sharh of Sahih Muslim), at-Taqreeb wa-Taiseer on Hadith. Rawdlathu-Taalibeen, Minhaj at-Taalibeen, al-Majmu Sharh al-Muhazzab, on Shafii Fiqh, at-Tahrir fi Alfaz at-Tanbeeh on Arabic language, and many more. He died on Rajab 676 H. (December 1277 AD.) at his hometown Nawa. Although he passed away almost a thousand years ago, his knowledge continues to benefit us to this very day, via the enormous contribution he has left behind to the Muslim Ummah. May Allah forgive him and be Pleased with him. Ameen.

Nawawis 40 Hadith is a comprehensive collection of Prophetic Hadith that cover the basic foundations of Islam in short, easy to read manner. Although brief, this work includes a range of topics such as: Islam, Iman (ie: faith), good deeds, Halal and Haram, brotherhood, charity, obedience, asceticism, manners, commanding good and forbidding evil, trusting in Allah, rememberance of Allah, and other important matters of religion. This famous compilation is continued to be taught and learned by Muslims, young and old, throughout history. And many renowned scholars have produced various commentaries and explanations based upon this book. It is recommended that students of knowledge begin memorizing Hadith by starting with these 40, before moving onto larger Hadith collections. Perhaps the reason for the wide spread acceptance of Nawawis book and most Muslims being familiar with it is his sincerity, and Allah knows best. Imam Nawawi has discussed his own book and the reasons for writing it in his introduction that follows. Although the exact number of Hadith that Imam Nawawi included was 42 , I have also mentioned after them the additional 8 Hadith that Ibn Rajab, the great Hanbali scholar, included in his commentary of Nawawis 40 Hadith, which is the best known explanation on this book to date, titled Jamiul Uloom wal-Hikam. I have explained some phrases within brackets, and given titles at the beginning of each Hadith, to make things easier for the reader.

The Prophet said: Whoever does not thank (and be grateful to) the people, does not thank (and is not grateful to) Allah. All praise and thanks be to Allah for granting the Thaufeeq (ie: success) to complete this book. I would also like to thank everyone who helped in producing this work, translating difficult Arabic phrases, correcting mistakes in the text, reviewing and checking everything, etc. May Allah give them constant abundant reward for their efforts. Any good this brings is from Allah, and any shortcomings are from ourselves and shaithan. May Peace and Blessings be upon our Prophet Muhammad.

Imam Nawawis Introduction


- In the Name of Allah, the Entirely Merciful, the Especially Merciful -

All praise (and thanks) is for Allah, the Rabb (ie: Lord) of the Aalameen (ie: creation), the One who sustains the Heavens and the Earths, the One who manages all of the creation, the One who sent Messengers, Allahs Peace and Blessings be upon them, to accountable beings (ie: humans), to guide them and explain to them the Sharia (ie: laws) of the religion, with decisive evidences and clear proofs. I praise Him for all of His Blessings, and I ask Him for more of His Favors and Generosity. And I testify that there is no God (truly worthy of worship) except Allah alone, the One, the Supreme, the Generous, the Forgiving. And I testify that our master Muhammad is His slave and Messenger, and His beloved and Khalil (ie: close friend), the best of the creation, the one honored with the noble Quran, the lasting miracle throughout all the years, and (sent) with his Sunnah that lights (the way) for those who seek guidance, the one who is unique with (his) Jawamiul Kalim (ie: concise speech) and easy religion. Allahs Peace and Blessings be upon him and all of the Prophets and Messengers, and each of their families and upon all of the righteous.

And the Huffaz (ie: Hadith scholars) have agreed that it is a weak Hadith, even if its chains are many. And the scholars, Allah be pleased with them, have indeed authored inummerable works in this subject (of 40 Hadith). And the first I know of who authored in it was Abdullah bin al-Mubaarak, then Muhammad bin Aslam at-Toosi the Rabbani (ie: firmly grounded) scholar, then Hasan bin Sufyan an-Nasai, and Abu Bakr al-Aajurri, and Abu Bakr Muhammad bin Ibrahim al-Asfahani, and Daraqutni, and Hakim, and Abu Nuaim, and Abu Abdurrahman as-Sulami, and Abu Saeed al-Maaleeni, and Abu Usman as-Saabooni, and Abdullah bin Muhammad al-Ansari, and Abu Bakr al-Baihaqi, and countless others from the earlier and later times.

And indeed, I have sought guidance from Allah, the Highest, in collecting 40 Hadith, emulating these eminent Imams and Huffaz (ie: preservers) of Islam. And indeed, the scholars have agreed upon the permissibility of acting upon weak Hadith in Fadlail al-Amaal (ie: encouraging good deeds). Even so, my basis (for this collection) is not upon this (weak) Hadith, but rather upon his saying, Peace and Blessings be upon him, in Sahih (authentic) Hadith: The one present among you should convey (what I say) to the one absent. And his saying, Peace and Blessings be upon him: May Allah brighten the (face of a) man who hears what I say and understands it, then conveys it just as he had heard it.

Then from the scholars were those who collected 40 (Hadith) on Usool ad-Deen (ie: fundamentals of religion), and some of them on Furoo (ie: secondary branches of religion), and some of them on Jihad (ie: warfare), and some of them on Zuhd (ie: asceticism), and some of them on manners, and some of them on Khutbas (sermons), and all of those are righteous purposes, may Allah be Pleased with those who intended them. And indeed, I see that a collection of 40 (Hadith) is more important than all of these, and that is 40 Hadith which encompasses all of that (mentioned before). And each Hadith from it is a great foundation from the foundations of religion, and indeed the scholars have described that Islam revolves around it, or it is half of Islam, or a third, and so on.

Afterwards, I oblige myself in (collecting) these 40 (Hadith) that they are to be Sahih (authentic), and most of them are in the two Sahihs, Bukhari and Muslim. And I mention them without their Sanads (ie: chains of narration), in order to make their memorization easy and their benefit widespread, if Allah the Highest Wills. Then I will follow them with a section on explaining their obscure phrases.

And it should be that everyone who desires the Hereafter should know these Hadith, because of what they encompass of important matters, and what they comprise in bringing to attention all obedience (to Allah). And that is clear to whoever thinks deeply about it. And upon Allah do I rely, and to Him is my trust and dependence, and for Him is all praise and blessings, and by Him is my success and protection from mistakes.

Abu Moosa al-Ashari narrated: The Prophet said: The example of the guidance and knowledge that Allah has sent me with, is like abundant rain that fell upon the earth. So from that land was good (fertile soil) that accepted (and absorbed) the water. So it grew lots of pasture and grass. And from that land was hard (soil) that held the water (for a while without absorbing it all up quickly). So Allah made the people benefit from it, and they drank it, made (their animals) drink from it, and irrigated their plants with it. And that water also fell upon another piece of land, which was only bare and barren. It could neither hold water nor grow pasture. So (the first) is the example of someone who understands Allah's religion and what Allah has sent me with (ie: the Quran and Sunnah), and so he learns (it himself) and teaches (it to others). And (the last) is the example of someone who did not even raise his head with it (ie: did not even bother with it), and did not accept the guidance of Allah that I have been sent with (ie: the Quran and Sunnah).

The Imam, the Habr (learned), the companion of the Prophet, and the son of his companion. His father was not older than him except by about 11 years. He became Muslim before his father. He has praiseworthy traits, virtues, and firm in knowledge and deeds. He conveyed from the Prophet comprehensive knowledge. He was from the first and foremost of the Muslims, one of the companions who narrated many Hadith. One of the 4 Abdullah Fuqahas, and one of the scholars and worshippers. Narrated 700 Hadith.

Name: Abdulla bin Busr bin Abu Busr

Nasab: Shami, Himsi, Salami, Qaisi, then Mazini

Kunya: Abu Busr, or Abu Bashar, or Abu Sofwan

Death: 82 or 87 or 88 or 96 H. in Sham.

The companion, the blessing of Sham, the settler of Homs. He was a small companion. He has few Hadith and short companionship. His two brothers 'Atiyya and as-Somaa and their father are companions.
